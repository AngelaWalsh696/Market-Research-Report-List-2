<p><ul><li>Biochemical Transfection</li><li>Physical Transfection</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The transfection reagent market is divided into two primary types: biochemical and physical transfection methods. Biochemical transfection involves the use of various chemical compounds, such as lipids and polymers, to facilitate the delivery of nucleic acids into cells. This method is often efficient for diverse cell types. In contrast, physical transfection encompasses techniques such as electroporation and microinjection, where physical forces or direct delivery are applied to introduce genetic material. Both methods cater to specific research needs and applications in biotechnology and medicine.</p></p>
